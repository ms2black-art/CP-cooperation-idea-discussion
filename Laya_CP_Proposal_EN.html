04 Opportunities

Two product lines,
one co-creation engine behind them.

The model centers on two executable product lines — each built on a core CP asset to create differentiated value in Laya’s market. The CPF·CPRAM·PIM R&D engine is the means that makes both run.

Product Line 01
CORE ASSET — CP/PIM authentic Thai-flavor heritage

Thai-flavor sauces

Led by Thai-style mayonnaise, extendable to other Thai flavors. The biggest lever — change the flavor, not the line: the same chicken-burger, rice-burger, fries and egg-roll line becomes a “Thai-flavor series” with one sauce swap. Sauce = scalable, retail-ready flavor IP.

Product Line 02
CORE ASSET — CP global shrimp supply chain

Seafood-protein mains

Led by the Golden Shrimp Cake Rice Burger; where regulation allows, extend to other Thai chicken burgers. CP is the world’s largest shrimp producer — seafood is the importable protein path that sidesteps meat-import limits while telling the “CP supply chain” story.

The Engine
CORE ASSET — CPF·CPRAM·PIM R&D

Joint co-creation (the means)

Not another product, but the mechanism that lets the two lines exist: Thai-flavor know-how, rapid prototyping, two-way launch. The MOU and resource sharing are the means that switch this engine on.

Feasibility Check
✓
Sauces / curry pastes / seasonings (non-meat) — Importable or locally produced once commercially sterilized and compliant with food-safety labeling.
✓
Shrimp / seafood (raw, cooked, breaded) — Importable, subject to item-by-item quarantine & food-safety inspection.
✓
Coconut milk · coconut products · rice · noodles — Plant-based, low import barrier.
△
Commercially sterilized meat-filled retort pouches — Conditionally feasible; case-by-case compliance with import rules.
×
Thai chicken / pork (incl. cooked, vacuum-packed) — Restricted by disease-control bans; not importable.
Execution path: for the Thai chicken burgers in Product Line 02, the required chicken and pork protein is produced locally by CP Group’s own support operation in Taiwan — compliant with Taiwan food-safety rules and fast to scale, bringing the partnership fully to ground.

06 Roadmap

Bring the value amplifier to ground, phase by phase.

The MOU, flavor co-creation and resource sharing are the means that realize the loop. Regulatory verification runs in parallel with R&D, confirming feasibility before production.

Phase 0M0–2

Strategic alliance

Sign the strategic MOU; define scope, IP & profit sharing, and governance; set up a joint working group. Lay the foundation first.

Phase 1M2–6

Joint R&D

PIM/CPRAM develop the Thai sauce platform and shrimp-cake burger; local trial production in Taiwan. Regulatory checks run in parallel (food safety, quarantine, shellfish labeling), with consumer testing in Laya stores.

Phase 2M6–9

Pilot & launch

Pilot in selected stores → full launch across 500 stores. Activate marketing to turn products into conversation and sales.

Phase 3M9–18+

Two-way return

Winning products go home to 7-Eleven / CP channels in Thailand; bottled sauces go retail; a standing co-development platform is set up; evaluate Laya’s entry into Southeast Asia via CP channels.
